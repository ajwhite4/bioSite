Bio

Jon White Jr. was born and raised in Omaha, NE, the oldest of three brothers. Growing up, he was active in Boy Scouts and Football in High School. In Boy Scouts, he was given the nickname “Crazy Spider,” and the nickname has since stuck. After High School, he went to the University of Wyoming, where he met his wife, Emily. They had a son named Austin.

Jon went through several interests over the years, from learning to play guitar on his own then creating an eBay store where he focused primarily on antiques and military souvenirs. He got hooked on the TV show Diggers on the National Geographic channel in 2014. Then, five years ago, one of Jon’s best friends added him to the “Detecting the Heartland” Facebook page. A short time later he decided to buy himself a metal detector and try it out.

Over time, Jon learned the best approaches to success, where researching the history of land determines if there will be anything to find in that area. Doing this helps you appreciate history, and he’s grown an appreciation and interest in cartography and collecting old coins. He also loves that metal detecting helps clean the environment where they can collect and recycle any trash they find. The mental exercise and light workout are just a bonus, just like any items he finds.

Once Jon got enough experience, he started a YouTube channel, Crazy Spider Adventures, to share his experience with others. As he became a little more known in the metal detecting community MineLab offered Jon a position to be one of their Detexperts (social influencers). Detecting the Heartland leaders also made him an administrator for their Facebook page.

His favorite finds are a 1900-O Barber half-dollar, a late 1800s micro mosaic brooch from Italy, and a Civil war breast and box plate. His favorite place he has been metal detecting was at a small acreage in northern Iowa along the Minnesota border. However, he has a trip to Alaska in July 2022 to detect some land next to land owned by TV stars Steve and Shawn Pomrenke from the show Bering Sea Gold on National Geographic.

He loves working with the younger generation to share his experience and help them understand the importance of metal detecting. He also enjoys being able to help people find lost items, even helping law enforcement find evidence of crimes. In addition, metal detecting has taught Jon a higher level of generosity by allowing him to give back to his local community, donating equipment to new metal detectorists, and volunteering through Detecting the Heartland.
